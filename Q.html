Grade Point Average (GPA) Calculation

GPA is the weighted average of Grade Points obtained in all courses completed by a student. For example, if a student has passed five courses in a term with the following credits and grade points:

Course Credit Grade Point
A 3 4.00
B 3 3.00
C 3 3.75
D 2 3.25
E 1 3.50

The GPA for the term is calculated as:

= (3*4.0 + 3*3.0 + 3*3.75 + 2*3.25 + 1*3.5) / (3 + 3 + 3 + 2 + 1) = 3.52

What is TGPA, YGPA and CGPA

A student's performance is evaluated in terms of three indices:

  • Term Grade Point Average (TGPA): Calculated by dividing the total grade points earned in a term by the number of credits taken in that term.
  • Yearly Grade Point Average (YGPA): Calculated by dividing the total grade points earned in two terms in a year by the total credits taken in that year.
  • Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA): Calculated by dividing the total grade points accumulated till date by the total completed credits.

For example, a student who has earned 275 grade points in attempting 100 credits of courses would have an overall CGPA of 2.75.

How to Use the TGPA Calculator?

The TGPA calculator is designed to help you estimate your term GPA based on your estimated marks for each subject. Follow these steps to use the calculator:

  1. Enter your estimated marks for each subject in the input fields provided.
  2. Click the "CALCULATE TGPA" button at the bottom of the form.
  3. Your estimated TGPA will be displayed on the screen.
